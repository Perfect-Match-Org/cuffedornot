THE METHOD

NO SHOT YOU ACTUALLY CLICKED THIS. You want to know how we "guess" your relationship status from your music stats. You're reading the fine print of a website that judges your love life based on your music taste. This is where you are in life right now. Wow.

HERE'S THE THING THOUGH — it's not completely made up!! We analyze the EMOTIONAL DNA of your music: valence (happiness), energy, danceability, and acousticness. These are the same audio features used in actual peer-reviewed music psychology research. Rentfrow & Gosling (2003), "The Do Re Mi's of Everyday Life" is literally THE foundational paper that proved music preferences predict personality traits and emotional states. Your music choices are basically a personality test you didn't know you were taking.

AND IT GETS WORSE (for you). Nave et al. (2018), "Musical Preferences Predict Personality" ran a massive study and confirmed that audio features like valence, energy, and danceability can infer your psychological traits. Low happiness + high acousticness + low danceability? That's textbook single-person listening. Don't be mad at us, be mad at the science. Garrido & Schubert (2011), "Individual Differences in the Enjoyment of Negative Emotion in Music" found that people experiencing sadness actively seek out sad music. You're probably doing it right now.

WE USE THE VALENCE-AROUSAL FRAMEWORK validated by Eerola & Vuoskoski (2011), "A comparison of the discrete and dimensional models of emotion in music" to map your audio features onto an emotional landscape. High valence + high energy = you're thriving (or delusional). Low valence + high energy = angry breakup anthems. Low everything = you're in your sad girl/boy era and we respect that. Ferrer et al. (2013) confirmed valence is a reliable measure of emotional content in music, which is why it carries the heaviest weight in our scoring.

WE ALSO LOOK AT how old your music is and how popular it is. Saarikallio & Erkkilä (2007), "The Role of Music in Adolescents' Mood Regulation" mapped how people use music to process emotions. Digging up old tracks is a nostalgia response. Going super niche is sometimes an isolation signal. But mostly we just think it's funny to judge you for still listening to your 2019 playlist.

THE FINAL SCORE blends your audio profile (75%), track age patterns (15%), and popularity signals (10%) into a number from 0 to 100. Zero means your music radiates "we share a Spotify account" energy. One hundred means your listening history is basically a therapy session transcript. Most people land somewhere in the middle because relationships (and music taste) are complicated.

Your stats come from your own Receiptify receipt — we never touch your Spotify account. Everything runs in your browser. Nothing gets stored. Nothing gets sent anywhere. We literally couldn't harvest your data if we wanted to (which we don't, because that sounds exhausting).

IS THIS SCIENTIFICALLY RIGOROUS? It's based on real research (see above). Is it more accurate than your friend who says "you'll find someone when you stop looking"? Almost certainly. We built this because we thought it would be funny. The fact that you're still reading this suggests we were right.
